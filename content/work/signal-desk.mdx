## Challenge

The existing portfolio material leaned heavily toward public-facing landing pages. I needed a third case study that could show product-grade structure, information density, and a more restrained premium tone.

## Role

This concept was built as a deliberate portfolio piece. The goal was not to invent fictional complexity everywhere, but to prove that I can shape overview, analytics, onboarding, and settings screens with the same attention to hierarchy, product logic, and polish.

## System

The concept is implemented as a static Next.js app with a reusable dashboard shell, shared card language, responsive tables, and distinct screen purposes for overview, projects, analytics, onboarding, and settings.

## Interactions

- The overview screen prioritizes signal over decoration by keeping KPIs, health summaries, and workload status within one scan.
- Dense areas such as tables and charts use contrast, spacing, and alignment to stay readable.
- Motion remains restrained and respects reduced-motion preferences to keep the UI product-grade.

## Implementation

- Shared app shell with navigation, status surfaces, and compact cards.
- Static datasets for metrics, task health, pipelines, and operational timelines.
- Consistent visual system from onboarding to settings so the concept feels like one product, not five screens.

## Outcome

- The portfolio gains a clear B2B / SaaS register.
- Prospects can now see both marketing polish and product discipline in the same body of work.
- It creates a ready-made concept repo that can evolve into more case studies or branch into client-specific adaptations.
